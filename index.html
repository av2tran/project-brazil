Brazil

In Brazil, there are huge levels of inequality even though it has one of the larest economies in the world. Currenly having the ninth-largest economy in the world and being the largest in Latin-America with their massive 1.85 trillion in GDP. Yet Brazil is one of the few countries that does not have decent education statistics. Brazil underperforms academically in math, science, and reading. The school's infrastructure and technology infrastructure are linked to the quality of the education. Teachers in Brazil, regardless of what grade they teach, their salaries are usually low compared to other countries.

It is known mostly everywhere that a degree helps you earn a higher salary than people who don’t have one. But, in Brazil, the gap between those who do and don’t have degrees is vastly great. An example would be regular graduate salaries are 2.5 higher than those without degrees, but for those who weren’t able to finish secondary education, the salaries end up being 5 times higher. That is the reason for the income inequality and educational difference. You might be asking why the difference between those who are educated versus those who aren’t so large. The sad truth is that Brazil’s education system favor’s the rich. Those who usually attend public universities are most likely to be white, rich, and have attended private schools in their past.
